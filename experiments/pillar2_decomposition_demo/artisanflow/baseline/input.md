## Venture Description

ArtisanFlow is a B2C SaaS workflow tool for independent craft sellers on Etsy, Shopify, and Faire — automating inventory reconciliation, cross-channel listings, and order routing so a solo maker can operate like a small warehouse without hiring. The product ingests order data via OAuth from each platform, normalizes SKUs, and writes pick-pack-ship instructions to a phone-optimized queue.

## Target Customer

Solo makers and 2–3 person craft businesses shipping 200–1,500 orders per month across two or more channels. Anti-ICP: sellers on a single channel (don't need reconciliation), dropshippers (don't handle inventory), enterprise fulfillment centers (need ERP-grade systems).

## Team

Two co-founders: a solo-operator with five years running her own craft business on three channels, and a backend engineer who previously shipped a Shopify app in the same segment. Both full-time. One part-time contractor handles support tickets.

## Market Evidence

Roughly 180,000 Etsy sellers gross over $50K/year and the top quartile run on 2+ channels. Zapier, Airtable, and Google Sheets are the current duct-tape stack; switching costs from them are low but opportunity cost of maintenance is high. No current vendor focuses specifically on the multi-channel solo maker.

## Traction Signal

Product in market for 14 months. 212 paying subscribers at $29/month Starter or $59/month Pro. Annual revenue run-rate $145K. Net churn negative for the last two quarters (expansion revenue from Starter → Pro upgrades exceeds cancellations). NPS 48. Featured in two independent newsletters for the segment.

## Unit Economics

Blended ARPU $41/month. CAC through newsletter sponsorships and SEO content: $68. Tenure average 14 months. LTV $574. LTV/CAC 8.4. Gross margin 87%. Infra cost per user $1.80/month; support cost $3.40/month.

## Gate Rule

Advance from Validate to Build requires: founder committed capital, evidence of demand, and first ventures actively using the platform. All conditions are met. Founder has committed full-time effort plus $60K personal capital. 212 paying subscribers are actively using the product today with annual revenue run-rate of $145K. Churn is net-negative and the product has reached a self-sustaining cash-flow profile for the founding team.
