## Venture Description

NorthPass is a consumer social app concept around "hiking buddies" — pairing nearby users by matching planned trail difficulty, pace, and available dates. The planned model is freemium with a paid tier for verified-identity matching and insurance-backed no-show guarantees.

## Target Customer

Casual and intermediate solo hikers in North American metro areas with easy trail access — Denver, Seattle, Boulder, Portland, Salt Lake. Target age 24–38. Anti-ICP: competitive trail runners (have clubs already), ultralight backpackers (solo by preference), families (existing family-trip channels).

## Team

Solo founder working part-time in evenings and weekends. No technical co-founder yet. No advisors signed on. Founder's day job is as a UX designer at an unrelated SaaS company.

## Venture Description

Envelop is an AI operating partner that takes domain experts — consultants, recruiters, ex-executives, niche operators — from business idea to honest verdict to personalized game plan to first revenue, in weeks not months. The core product is a simplified Gantt-style workspace showing the entrepreneur one path and one Next Best Action (NBA) at all times. AI agents handle ~90% of execution tasks. The funnel is: free assessment → $49 report unlock (10% convert) → $149–$449/month subscription (10% convert) → $12 extra actions (~6/subscriber/month).

## Market Evidence

Hiking participation grew from 47M to 59M US adults between 2015 and 2023 (Outdoor Foundation). No breakout app currently occupies the "activity-partner matching" space for hiking specifically; existing apps focus on route-finding (AllTrails) or general activity partners (Meetup) but not on the matching problem. Whether this is a real gap or a proven non-market is not yet clear from the current evidence.

## Market Evidence

Roughly 180,000 Etsy sellers gross over $50K/year and the top quartile run on 2+ channels. Zapier, Airtable, and Google Sheets are the current duct-tape stack; switching costs from them are low but opportunity cost of maintenance is high. No current vendor focuses specifically on the multi-channel solo maker.

## Traction Signal

No product in market. No waitlist collected. No user interviews conducted with the target persona. Landing page mocked in Figma but not deployed. Founder has discussed the idea with three friends, two of whom were enthusiastic and one of whom was skeptical.

## Market Evidence

$12B K-12 tutoring market. Human tutors are expensive and scarce. No existing product combines real-time handwriting analysis with a voice AI tutor in a single integrated experience. Personalized AI-generated content is a planned differentiator.

## Unit Economics

Pricing, costs, and tenure have not been modeled. Planned freemium conversion assumed at 3–5% based on category averages cited in blog posts. Infrastructure costs not estimated. Unit economics are a projection at the mid-single-digit-percent conversion level; nothing has been validated against real conversion data.

## Gate Rule

Advance from Validate to Build requires: problem validated with paying or committed users, evidence that the matching experience is meaningfully better than existing alternatives, and at least one distribution channel with demonstrated conversion. None of these conditions are currently met. No users. No validation. No committed customers. No distribution channel tested at any volume.
