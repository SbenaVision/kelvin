## Venture Description

Meridian is a B2B SaaS tool for regional commercial banks' loan operations teams — specifically, a collateral-monitoring dashboard that tracks property, equipment, and accounts-receivable pledges across a bank's portfolio and flags covenant-breach risk before it surfaces in the quarterly review. The product ingests loan-book data from the bank's core (Fiserv, Jack Henry) and layers in external signals (property-tax delinquency, UCC filings, industry benchmarks).

## Target Customer

Mid-sized commercial banks with portfolios of $500M–$5B in commercial loans. Target buyer is the Chief Credit Officer or VP of Loan Operations. Anti-ICP: credit unions under $200M (don't have the portfolio complexity), national banks over $50B (have in-house tooling already).

## Team

Three founders: ex-CCO from a $2B regional bank, a data engineer from a fintech that integrated with the same cores, and a product lead from a compliance-tech startup. All full-time. One advisor from a regional banking industry group.

## Unit Economics

Pricing, costs, and tenure have not been modeled. Planned freemium conversion assumed at 3–5% based on category averages cited in blog posts. Infrastructure costs not estimated. Unit economics are a projection at the mid-single-digit-percent conversion level; nothing has been validated against real conversion data.

## Market Evidence

Roughly 900 target banks in the US. Each typically spends $150K–$400K/year on risk tooling. The dominant incumbents (Abrigo, Baker Hill) are priced and architected for larger banks; smaller regionals use spreadsheets plus the quarterly manual review. Two industry analyst reports identify covenant monitoring as an under-served operational gap in this segment.

## Traction Signal

Product in market for 14 months. 212 paying subscribers at $29/month Starter or $59/month Pro. Annual revenue run-rate $145K. Net churn negative for the last two quarters (expansion revenue from Starter → Pro upgrades exceeds cancellations). NPS 48. Featured in two independent newsletters for the segment.

## Traction Signal

Two signed design-partner Letters of Intent at target $180K ACV (12-month paid pilots starting next quarter). Four additional banks in active discovery conversations, two of which have requested security questionnaires. Product is in private beta with one of the LOI partners running synthetic data. No paid revenue yet.

## Venture Description

FreakinGenius (FG) is an AI-powered math tutoring service for middle school students who struggle with math. The product is a dual experience: an AI voice chatbot that replaces the human tutor, paired with a tablet app that analyzes the student's handwritten work in real-time and feeds signals back to the chatbot. All interaction between the student and the AI is voice-based.

## Unit Economics

Target ACV $180K per customer at blended margin 78%. CAC projected $55K per logo (outbound sales plus conference presence). Churn expected 5% annually at steady-state. Gross-margin LTV projected $2.3M per logo over a 7-year tenure assumption. Current burn $85K/month on a $1.1M seed round closed three months ago.

## Gate Rule

Advance from Validate to Build requires: founder committed capital, evidence of demand, and first ventures actively using the platform. Some conditions are met. Founders are full-time and a seed round is closed. Two LOIs are in hand at target ACV but paid contracts have not been countersigned and no revenue has been recognized. The private beta is running with synthetic data only — no production customer traffic through the platform yet.
